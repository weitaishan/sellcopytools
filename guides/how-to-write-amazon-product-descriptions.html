How to Write Amazon Product Descriptions & Bullet Points

Writing copy for an Amazon listing is different from writing for your own Shopify store. Amazon shoppers are highly comparison-driven, read mostly on mobile devices, and search via a specific keyword algorithm (A9). To succeed, your listing must satisfy both the search algorithm and human buyers.

The Anatomy of an Amazon Listing

A complete Amazon product listing contains three primary copy blocks that you control in Seller Central:

  • Product Title: The most important factor for SEO ranking and click-through rate.
  • Bullet Points (Key Product Features): Five scannable statements below the price where purchase decisions are made.
  • Product Description: A longer text block below the fold (replaced by A+ Content for brand-registered sellers).

1. How to Write Amazon Bullet Points

Bullet points are the most read text on your listing. Buyers use them to scan for specific features, materials, and sizes. Follow these guidelines:

The Bullet Point Rule

[CAPITALIZED BENEFIT]: Explanatory sentence showing feature + real-world solution. Keep it under 200 characters.

  • Lead with a capitalized hook: Start each bullet point with 2-4 capitalized words that summarize the benefit (e.g. "SPILL-PROOF DESIGN", "FAST USB-C CHARGING").
  • Keep them concise: Limit each bullet point to 150-250 characters. Long blocks of text look overwhelming on mobile devices.
  • Address core objections: Use the five bullets to cover compatibility, material durability, what is included in the box, dimensions, and warranty info.

2. Writing the Product Description

If you do not have Amazon A+ Content (formerly EBC), your product description is a plain text block. Amazon has strict rules regarding what you can and cannot include:

  • Avoid HTML tags: Amazon officially prohibits HTML tags (like bold or lists) in standard descriptions. Only basic line breaks (<br>) are commonly used, though even those are technically discouraged by Amazon's strict policy.
  • Do not include promotional claims: Never mention sales, discounts, free shipping, or temporary prices.
  • Focus on specifications: Use this section to outline full package contents, setup steps, care instructions, and specific materials.

3. Amazon Listing Copy Example

Here is an example of optimized Amazon copy for an ergonomic desk mat:

Amazon Bullet Points Example

• WATERPROOF & EASY TO CLEAN: The water-resistant surface protects your wooden desk from coffee spills, tea drops, and stains. Simply wipe it clean with a damp cloth.
• SMOOTH MOUSE GLIDE: Made from premium PU leather, this desk pad offers a smooth tracking surface for both optical and laser mice, reducing wrist fatigue.
• NON-SLIP BACKING: The anti-skid suede base prevents the mat from sliding during intense typing sessions, gaming, or daily office work.
• LARGE DESK COVERAGE: Measuring 31" x 15" (80 x 40 cm), the pad is large enough to fit your laptop, mechanical keyboard, mouse, and daily workspace accessories.
• STITCHED EDGES: Reinforced anti-fray stitched borders ensure the mat will not curl or peel, delivering long-term durability for home office setups.

Amazon Description Example

Upgrade your home office workspace with a durable, double-sided PU leather desk pad. Designed to protect your desk from scratches, ink stains, and heat, this minimalist mat provides a comfortable writing and mouse surface.

Made from premium synthetic leather, the mat is hypoallergenic and safe for wood, glass, and metal desk tops. The smooth surface makes mouse tracking effortless, while the textured backing stays secure. Ideal for remote workers, designers, and gamers.

Package Includes:
1 x PU Leather Desk Pad (31" x 15")
1 x PU Leather Storage Strap

4. Amazon SEO and Keywords

Amazon's A9 search engine matches search terms directly with words in your listing. Avoid keyword stuffing. Instead, place keywords strategically:

  • Title: Place your brand name and your main high-volume keyword at the very beginning.
  • Bullet Points: Distribute secondary long-tail keywords naturally across the five bullets.
  • Backend Search Terms: Put spelling variations, synonyms, and other keywords in the Seller Central backend search terms (do not repeat words already in your visible listing).
