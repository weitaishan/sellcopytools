Why product bullets matter

Most shoppers do not read every sentence on a product page. Bullets help them understand the offer quickly. The strongest bullets connect a feature to a benefit, so the buyer sees why the detail matters.

  • Keep each bullet focused on one idea.
  • Use concrete details instead of vague claims.
  • Lead with benefits when the audience is scanning fast.
  • Group similar details together when possible.
  • Use plain language that matches the product category.

Feature-to-benefit examples

Instead of writing “stainless steel body,” write “stainless steel body for everyday durability.” Instead of “adjustable strap,” write “adjustable strap for a more comfortable fit.” The feature gives proof; the benefit gives the shopper a reason to care.
