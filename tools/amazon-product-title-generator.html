Amazon product title basics

Amazon titles need to be readable, accurate, and specific. A strong title usually combines the product type with practical details shoppers compare, such as size, quantity, material, color, use case, or compatibility.

  • Start with the product category or product type.
  • Add the strongest identifying details.
  • Use natural wording instead of keyword stuffing.
  • Keep claims factual and supportable.
  • Make sure the title matches the images, bullets, and description.

Amazon title examples

Instead of “Water Bottle,” try a more complete draft like “Insulated Stainless Steel Water Bottle, 24 oz, Leakproof Lid for Gym and Travel.” The better version gives shoppers more information without feeling confusing.

FAQ

What should an Amazon product title include?

Include the product type, the most important feature, size or quantity when relevant, and a clear use case. Keep the wording accurate and easy to scan.

Should I put every keyword in the title?

No. Put the strongest product phrase and key comparison details in the title. Save supporting phrases for bullets, description, backend search terms, and A+ content.

Can this tool write titles for private-label products?

Yes. It works well for private-label drafts as long as you provide specific product details such as material, size, compatibility, use case, and package contents.

What to avoid

Avoid title drafts that repeat the same phrase, make unsupported claims, or hide the product type behind marketing language. Shoppers should understand the item before they read the bullets.
