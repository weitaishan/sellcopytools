What a landing page headline needs

Your headline should tell the visitor what the product helps them do and why it matters. It does not need to explain every detail; it needs to make the page worth continuing.

  • Name the desired outcome clearly.
  • Make the audience or use case obvious when helpful.
  • Avoid vague lines that could fit any product.
  • Keep the promise aligned with proof lower on the page.
  • Test clarity before cleverness.

Headline formulas

Try “Get [outcome] without [friction],” “The easier way to [goal],” “Built for [audience] who need [benefit],” or “[Product category] designed for [use case].” Use the formula as a starting point, then make it specific.

How to choose the best headline

Pick the version that matches the traffic source. Search traffic may need direct keyword clarity, while paid social traffic may need a stronger problem-solution angle. The headline and ad should feel like one continuous promise.
