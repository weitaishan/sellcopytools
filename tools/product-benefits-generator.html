Feature versus benefit

A feature explains what the product has. A benefit explains why that detail matters to the buyer. Strong ecommerce copy usually needs both: the benefit creates interest, and the feature provides proof.

  • Start with the customer problem or desired outcome.
  • Connect each feature to a real use case.
  • Write benefits in plain language.
  • Avoid exaggerated promises that the product cannot support.
  • Use benefit ideas in bullets, headlines, emails, and ads.

Benefit examples

“Adjustable brightness” becomes “set the right light for focused work or relaxed reading.” “Machine washable cover” becomes “clean spills quickly without replacing the product.” The better version explains the buyer outcome.

Editing tip

After generating benefits, remove anything that sounds generic. Keep the ideas that are specific to the product, audience, and shopping situation.
