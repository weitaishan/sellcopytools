What makes a useful slogan?

A useful slogan is memorable because it is specific to the brand promise. It should hint at the audience, benefit, feeling, or category without sounding like it could belong to any company.

  • Keep the line short and easy to say.
  • Connect to a real brand benefit.
  • Avoid vague phrases like “quality you can trust” unless supported by a stronger idea.
  • Test how it looks beside the brand name.
  • Check for confusing meanings in your target markets.

Slogan directions to try

Try benefit-led, audience-led, category-led, emotional, minimalist, and campaign-specific directions. For a new store, clarity usually beats cleverness. For an established brand, a slogan can be more emotional or distinctive.

Before using a slogan

Search for similar slogans, check trademark risk where relevant, and test the line with real customers. A generator can help create options, but final brand language deserves careful review.
