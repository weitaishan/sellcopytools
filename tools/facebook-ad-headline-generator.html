What makes a Facebook ad headline work?

A good Facebook ad headline is simple, specific, and easy to understand beside the creative. It should reinforce the product benefit instead of trying to explain the entire offer in one line.

  • Lead with a clear customer outcome.
  • Match the headline to the image or video creative.
  • Use one angle per variation.
  • Avoid exaggerated claims or restricted language.
  • Test benefit, offer, curiosity, and social proof angles.

Useful headline angles

Try angles like “made for busy mornings,” “your desk just got easier,” “a better way to pack light,” or “the gift they will actually use.” The best angle depends on the audience, product maturity, and where the shopper is in the funnel.

Testing tip

Generate several headline options, then pair each one with a different creative concept. Do not judge headlines in isolation; paid social performance depends on the combination of creative, offer, landing page, and audience.
